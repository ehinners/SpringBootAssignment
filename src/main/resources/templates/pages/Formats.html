The Many Ways to Play

Some rules are flexible, and can be changed to cater each game to a different experience. Some established rulesets have become standardized and recognized by players as "formats". For example, which cards you are allowed to put in your deck changes drastically in each format, and is usually each format's defining factor. The "Standard" format, allows only newer cards, has a lower limit of at least 60 cards in a deck, is played 1 v 1, and with a starting life total of 20. Most formats only allow 4 copies of a card per deck, with the exception being basic lands. You may put any number of basic lands in your deck in most formats.

There are some exciting formats out there, and you're encouraged to try them whenever you can! Draft is a special format where decks are constructed on the spot, one card at a time from packs that are shared from a group of players. Commander is a format where you can only have 1 copy of a card per deck, have 100 card decks, and have a special designated "general" card that grants special abilities, and can be played at any time!
